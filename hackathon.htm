Objective: Encourage innovation, problem-solving, and logical thinking. Help participants develop real-world technical skills.

General Guidelines

Evaluation Criteria

  • Code functionality and correctness.
  • Complexity and innovation in solving challenges.
  • Clean and readable code.

Sample Challenge

Text Justification Problem: Given an array of strings, format the text such that each line has exactly maxWidth characters and is fully justified. Distribute extra spaces evenly, and left-align the last line.

FAQs - Track 1

  • Q: What type of projects can be submitted? A: Projects related to real-world problems, preferably solving issues in areas like healthcare, education, or automation.
  • Q: Can teams collaborate on the same project? A: Yes, teams of up to 4 people can collaborate and submit a single project.
  • Q: Are external libraries allowed? A: Yes, external libraries are allowed, but most questions should be solvable without them.

Track 2: Arduino-based Circuit Hackathon (Grades 9-10)

Introduction to Subevent: This track assesses participants' skills in designing and coding Arduino-based solutions using Tinkercad. It focuses on practical problem-solving and technical proficiency in circuit design and simulation.

Objective: Participants will be provided with tasks to design and simulate circuits using Tinkercad and submit functional solutions for evaluation.

General Guidelines

  • Eligibility: Open to high school students (Grade 9 and 10) with beginner to intermediate C++ Arduino skills.
  • Submission: Submit the task via Tinkercad with a link provided during the event.
  • All original work; AI tools prohibited.

Evaluation Criteria

Criteria Weightage
Flow of circuit design on Tinkercad 30%
Accuracy of the code 40%
Use of components 10%
Overall functionality 20%

Sample Challenge

Task 1: Design an Arduino-controlled robotic system that reacts to objects detected by a distance sensor. The system should have three different behaviors based on the distance.

Awards

Prizes and Recognition for top performers.

FAQs - Track 2

  • Q: Will Tinkercad simulations be evaluated based on hardware availability? A: No, Tinkercad offers all required components virtually, and simulation will be evaluated based on design, code, and functionality.
  • Q: Can I use other simulation tools? A: Only Tinkercad submissions will be accepted for this competition.
  • Q: Is prior Arduino knowledge required? A: Yes, participants should have a basic understanding of C++ and Arduino systems.
